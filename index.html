📋 Tournament rules & pairing logic

1. Team pairing logic

Teams are formed in this order of preference:

  1. Mixed — one man and one woman (preferred).
  2. Men — two men, if mixed pairs are not possible.
  3. Ladies — two women, only when no mixed or men pairing remains.

Within each step, partners are balanced by level: 2+ levels apart first, then 1 level, then similar levels so everyone plays. Team average levels are kept as even as possible across the draw.

2. Pools & courts

  • Teams are split into 3 pools, one per court (Courts 1, 2, and 3).
  • Pools are balanced by overall team strength (snake draft).
  • Each pool plays a round-robin; scores feed the standings and playoffs.

3. Match handicaps

Starting points go to the weaker side based on average team level:

  • 0.5 level difference → +2 points
  • 1.0 level difference (or more) → +3 points

Mixed pair vs two men:

  • Same average level → mixed pair +2
  • Mixed pair lower average → mixed pair +3
  • Mixed pair higher average → men’s pair +1

Each match is scored as 1 set by default. Use + Add set on a match to record 2 or 3 sets if needed.

4. Playoffs (your choice)

After group stage, pick a format based on time available:

  • Semifinals — top 4 (3 pool winners + best 2nd) → two semis → final (~3 matches).
  • Super 6 — top 2 from each pool (6 teams) → two groups of 3 (Courts 1 & 2) → pool winners meet in the final (~7 matches).
